Introduction to Harvest

EAs use Harvest to log hours on client accounts. You'll also use it to bill for new EA set-up, internal Base requests, and vacation coverage for fellow EAs.

💰
You get paid properly

Every hour you log is an hour you're compensated for.

📊
You can see where your time goes

Harvest data supports informed conversations with your client about priorities.

📋
Clients get reliable reports

Base sends monthly productivity reports on the 7th — powered by your Harvest data.

General Guidelines

  • Work in blocks. Minimize interruptions to log larger chunks of focused time.
  • Don't stop your timer between projects. 30 seconds per transition adds up fast.
  • Review daily. One minute a day keeps missing entries from piling up.

💡 Add the Harvest browser extension and desktop app for easy one-click time tracking.

Categorizing and Naming Your Projects

  • Project — Every Base client has a separate Project. Always log to the right one.
  • Task — Use dropdowns to differentiate work type. Need a new category? Email talent@basehq.com.
  • Notes — Be specific but concise. "Boston Conference Logistics" covers flight, car, and hotel — no need for three entries. Add a note to every task.

💡 If your note captures the purpose of the work, it's specific enough.

Managing Your Client's Block

  • Clarify preferences upfront. Is your client comfortable going over their block?
  • Review weekly. Flag concerns on check-in calls before they become issues.
  • Communicate overages before they happen. Never continue work without client awareness.
  • Estimate with a 25% buffer when giving timelines to your client.
  • Don't work for free. Log every hour. Confirm overages before proceeding.

💡 Overcommunication about time is always better than a surprise overage.

Creating a Detailed Time Report

  1. Click Reports, then Detailed Time.
  2. Choose client(s), or leave blank to see all projects.
  3. Filter by project and task as needed.
  4. Select yourself under Team to see only your hours.
  5. Check Include archived items for older projects.
  6. Click Run Report.

💡 Base sends monthly productivity reports on the 7th of each month. Your Harvest data powers them.

How Base Invoices Clients

Clients are invoiced on the first business day of each month for pre-paid hour blocks (20–160+ hrs). Unused hours don't roll over. Overages bill the following month. All hours must be logged by EOD on the last day of the month.

💡 Always confirm client approval before working additional time beyond their block.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why do we use Harvest if we also invoice separately?
Harvest powers accurate monthly invoicing, productivity reports, and seamless project coverage across Base EAs without billing delays.
Is time spent approving my invoice billable?
No — as a 1099 contractor, invoicing is a service for your business, not the client. Since invoices auto-generate through Wingspan, it typically takes just a few minutes.

Getting Started with Wingspan

Wingspan is Base's invoicing platform for contractor payments, plus access to health insurance and financial services. Once matched with your first client, look for this email:

📧 "Base invited you to onboard via Wingspan" — Your invitation link is inside. Don't skip it.

  1. Click Get Started in the invitation email.
  2. Create your account and enter all required information.
  3. Add your SSN or Tax ID# (required even with an LLC or Sole Partnership).

💡 Already have a Wingspan account? Your setup will skip the info-gathering step.

How Wingspan Works

1st
1st of each month

Your Harvest hours are sent to Base and Wingspan. Your invoice becomes available to review.

10th
Deadline — 10th of each month

Log into Wingspan and click Accept Invoice. Missing this deadline may push payment back by one month.

15th
15th of the following month

Payment is issued. Example: July hours are paid on August 15th.

📅 Set a recurring calendar event or Slackbot reminder around the 8th so you never miss the approval deadline.

Disputes & One-Time Payments

If your invoice is incorrect, select Dispute Invoice instead of Accept. Write a detailed message — it goes directly to Base Accounting. Once corrected, return to Wingspan to accept.

⚠️ Disputes filed close to the 10th deadline may cause a payment delay.

Success payments are added automatically — no action needed. Reset Specialists: email talent@basehq.com with your name, assignment, and payment amount once complete.

Base's Matching Process

Here's a step-by-step look at how you go from expressing interest to getting matched with a client.

1
Mark your interest

Log into the talent portal and mark your interest in any clients you'd like to work with. There's no limit. Our Talent team will only present you to one active client at a time unless you've discussed otherwise.

2
The Talent team identifies top matches

Our team reviews your profile, interest statement, and other factors — time zone, areas of expertise, client preferences — to determine the best fits.

3
The client reviews two EA profiles

The client receives two profiles from our Talent team and decides whether to request a GTKY call. The client may also decline both and request new options at any time.

4
GTKY calls take place

If selected by the client, you'll attend a 15-minute Get-To-Know-You (GTKY) call. See the GTKY Calls tab for more on how to prepare.

5
The client makes their final selection

After GTKY calls, the client selects their EA — or requests new options until they're ready to move forward.

What Happens After?

✅
If you're selected

You'll receive an email notification. Click through to accept the opportunity before moving forward. Once accepted, the Talent team sets up your tools and sends the New Client Onboarding Checklist.

💛
If you're not chosen

You'll still receive an email. You'll remain in the talent cloud and are encouraged to keep marking interest in other clients. Every EA in the cloud is exceptional — please don't be discouraged.

Get-To-Know-You (GTKY) Calls

GTKY calls are a key part of our matching process. These brief, 15-minute conversations are designed to help both you and the client determine whether the partnership feels like the right fit — gauging chemistry, communication style, and overall alignment before anyone commits.

🗓
Format

15 minutes, video call. Keep it conversational — this is a mutual interview, not a formal presentation.

🤝
Purpose

Chemistry and communication fit matter as much as skills. These calls ensure the match works for both sides.

💬
If it doesn't feel right

That's okay. These calls exist to protect your time and theirs. A good fit only works when it works for both people.

📋 Preparing for your GTKY call? Review the GTKY Call Guide for tips on how to show up confidently and make the most of your 15 minutes.

Contract Renewals & Rate Negotiations

At Base, we're committed to fair, transparent, and competitive compensation. Your rate is based on a consistent, predictable system — not on how well you negotiate. We welcome conversations about pay, and we'll always prioritize maintaining standard criteria for increases.

⚖️
Fair

No negotiation games or forced rankings. Everyone operates on the same consistent criteria.

🔍
Transparent

We operate without secrets. Your earnings are private — shared only when necessary.

📖
Easily Understood

Everyone should know what affects their rate and how to progress over time.

💰
Competitive

Your compensation is on par with or better than similar roles elsewhere.

How to Request a Rate Negotiation

If you're negotiating as part of your annual contract renewal, reply directly to your anniversary email. If you're negotiating out of cycle, email talent@basehq.com. In either case, include: your name, client name(s) and monthly block size(s), length of relationship with Base and client(s), your hourly rate request, evidence of exceptional performance, a summary of why you feel you deserve a rate increase, and whether you're comfortable with Base contacting your client to confirm feedback (Y/N).

Contract Renewals

Assuming a satisfactory relationship between Base, your client(s), and you, we'll invite you to renew your contract annually around your anniversary of starting with your first Base client.

Once a Rate Increase Is Approved

Base submits a ticket to update your rate. The change will appear on your invoice (viewable in Wingspan on the 1st of the following month). If there's an error, dispute it with reasoning and we'll review and reissue as needed.

💬 Pay questions? Email talent@basehq.com.

Client Travel Policy

Base doesn't require travel, but supports it when mutually beneficial. Before any trip, align clearly with your client on expectations, expenses, and availability. Clients are responsible for all travel-related costs.

Before You Travel

Set expectations early. Cover: flying or driving? (If driving and unable to work, bill transit time in Harvest.) Will there be downtime for other clients? Any additional work-related expenses? Does your client have a formal travel policy, per diem, or budget?

✈️ Flights & Transport

Book via the client's card. Consider linking it to Uber or Lyft for single-use access.

🚗 Mileage

Reimbursed at the IRS standard rate. If claiming mileage, do not also request fuel reimbursement.

🏨 Hotel

Confirm budget upfront. You may need to present your own card for incidentals — these are not reimbursable.

🍽 Meals

Follow the client's per diem or guidance. Be respectful with client-funded purchases.

🔁 Change Fees

Clients cover changes caused by circumstances outside your control.

👥 Multi-Client Days

Treat travel days like time off for your other clients. Arrange coverage as needed.

Client Transitions

Base's policy is that any EA wishing to end a client engagement provides a 2-week notice directly to Base — before informing the client. We prioritize troubleshooting before approving a transition, but if a transition is needed, we'll support you through it.

EA-Initiated Transitions

1
Inform Base first

Email talent@basehq.com with your client's name and company, how long you've worked together, your reason for transitioning, and your ideal last day.

2
Decide when to notify your client

We strongly encourage you to share the news directly — typically during a check-in call. Let talent@basehq.com and success@basehq.com know when you plan to do so.

3
Notify your client

Cover your last day, any outstanding projects you'll complete, and next steps with Base.

4
Schedule a client call with Base

Use the booking link to schedule a 30-minute call between your client and Base's Success team to discuss next steps.

5
Create an EA–Client Transition Guide

Review and update your client documentation — communication preferences, working style, calendar preferences, SOPs, and general notes. The incoming EA will receive this document.

6
Complete the transition

Base will begin matching the client with a new EA and will remove you from the client's Harvest project and Base software once the transition is complete.

💬 Transitions can be tough. We're here for you throughout. Reach out to talent@basehq.com anytime.
