Commitments

Your Commitments as a Base EA

We expect you to honor your agreements with Base and the executives you serve. If you foresee any issue meeting a deadline, communicate ahead of time. Mistakes happen — what matters is how you respond to them.

  • Block fulfillment. Fill your client's monthly block in full, every month. If more time is needed, always communicate before going over.
  • Minimum commitment. Commit to at least 4 months with each new client.
  • Time tracking. Log all hours worked in Harvest.
  • Onboarding plan. Complete the First 30 Days Onboarding Plan when matched with a new client.
  • Client paperwork. Be prepared to sign NDAs or data security agreements as needed.
  • Weekly updates. Share hours used with your client weekly.
  • Transitions. If leaving a Base client, inform Base first and follow the transition process.
  • Stay engaged. Participate in the Base Slack community to stay connected and keep growing.
  • Build your profile. We strongly encourage at least one client testimonial on your profile.

The 5-Step Matching Process

1
Mark your interest

Log into the talent portal and mark your interest in any clients you'd like to work with. There's no limit — our Talent team will only present you to one active client at a time unless you've discussed otherwise.

2
The Talent team identifies top matches

Our team reviews your profile, interest statement, time zone, areas of expertise, and client preferences to determine the best fits.

3
The client reviews two EA profiles

The client receives two profiles from our Talent team and decides whether to request a GTKY call. The client may also decline both and request new options at any time.

4
Get-To-Know-You (GTKY) calls

If selected by the client, you'll attend a 15-minute GTKY call. These brief conversations gauge chemistry, communication style, and alignment before anyone commits. If it doesn't feel right — that's okay. These calls exist to ensure a mutual fit.

5
The client makes their final selection

After GTKY calls, the client selects their EA — or requests new options until they're ready to move forward.

What Happens After?

✅
If you're selected

You'll receive an email notification. Click through to accept the opportunity before moving forward. Once accepted, the Talent team sets up your tools and sends the New Client Onboarding Checklist.

💛
If you're not chosen

You'll still receive an email. You'll remain in the talent cloud and are encouraged to keep marking interest in other clients. Every EA in the cloud is exceptional — please don't be discouraged.

Kick-Off Timeline

1
Accept your offer

Click through the offer email and accept. The Talent team will set up your tools and send the New Client Onboarding Checklist.

2
Receive your introduction

Base will introduce you to your executive via email. From this point, scheduling the Kick-Off call is your responsibility.

3
Schedule the Kick-Off call

Reply to the introduction email to confirm day and time, then send a calendar invite to your executive with a video conferencing link included.

4
You're off

We completely trust you to manage your client and your work. The Talent team is always available if you need support.

💬 Questions during onboarding? Reach out to talent@basehq.com — we're always happy to help.

Headshot Guidelines

A professional headshot is a key part of making a strong first impression. Here's what to keep in mind:

📐 Framing
  • Chest up, looking directly at the camera
  • Even lighting on your face, no shadows
🖼 Background
  • Clean, neutral background
  • Plain wall or nature work well
  • Avoid distracting elements
💡 Lighting
  • Well-lit location
  • Indoors: avoid backlighting
  • Outdoors: find "open shade"
👔 Attire
  • Office-appropriate clothing
  • Avoid busy textures or logos
  • Keep accessories minimal
📁 Format
  • High-resolution (300 ppi)
  • JPEG or PNG, max 8MB
  • Ideal: 600–800px

The first 30 days are crucial in establishing a strong EA–Executive relationship. This course, available on Teachable, is designed to help you hit the ground running.

📚
Course Access

Bill ~3 hours to Base (Base Internal Requests → First 30 Days Course) for taking the course, and ~3 hours to your client for deliverable work.

3
Summary of Accomplishments

Shared at the end of your first 30 days. An annotated version of your 30-Day Action Plan showing what was completed.

Harvest

Time tracking & billing

Open Harvest ↗

Introduction to Harvest

EAs use Harvest to log hours on client accounts. You'll also use it to bill for new EA set-up, internal Base requests, and vacation coverage for fellow EAs.

💰
You get paid properly

Every hour you log is an hour you're compensated for.

📊
See where your time goes

Harvest data supports informed conversations with your client about priorities.

📋
Clients get reliable reports

Base sends monthly productivity reports on the 7th — powered by your Harvest data.

General Guidelines

  • Work in blocks. Minimize interruptions to log larger chunks of focused time.
  • Don't stop your timer between projects. 30 seconds per transition adds up fast.
  • Review daily. One minute a day keeps missing entries from piling up.
💡 Add the Harvest browser extension and desktop app for easy one-click time tracking.

Categorizing and Naming Your Projects

  • Project — Every Base client has a separate Project. Always log to the right one.
  • Task — Use dropdowns to differentiate work type. Need a new category? Email talent@basehq.com.
  • Notes — Be specific but concise. "Boston Conference Logistics" covers flight, car, and hotel — no need for three entries. Add a note to every task.
💡 If your note captures the purpose of the work, it's specific enough.

Managing Your Client's Block

  • Clarify preferences upfront. Is your client comfortable going over their block?
  • Review weekly. Flag concerns on check-in calls before they become issues.
  • Communicate overages before they happen. Never continue work without client awareness.
  • Estimate with a 25% buffer when giving timelines to your client.
  • Don't work for free. Log every hour. Confirm overages before proceeding.
💡 Overcommunication about time is always better than a surprise overage.

Creating a Detailed Time Report

  1. Click Reports, then Detailed Time.
  2. Choose client(s), or leave blank to see all projects.
  3. Filter by project and task as needed.
  4. Select yourself under Team to see only your hours.
  5. Check Include archived items for older projects.
  6. Click Run Report.
💡 Base sends monthly productivity reports on the 7th of each month. Your Harvest data powers them.

How Base Invoices Clients

Clients are invoiced on the first business day of each month for pre-paid hour blocks (20–160+ hrs). Unused hours don't roll over. Overages bill the following month. All hours must be logged by EOD on the last day of the month.

💡 Always confirm client approval before working additional time beyond their block.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why do we use Harvest if we also invoice separately?

Harvest powers accurate monthly invoicing, productivity reports, and seamless project coverage across Base EAs without billing delays.

Is time spent approving my invoice billable?

No — as a 1099 contractor, invoicing is a service for your business, not the client. Since invoices auto-generate through Wingspan, it typically takes just a few minutes.

Wingspan

Invoicing, payments & benefits

Open Wingspan ↗

Getting Started with Wingspan

Wingspan is Base's invoicing platform for contractor payments, plus access to health insurance and financial services. Once matched with your first client, look for this email:

📧 "Base invited you to onboard via Wingspan" — Your invitation link is inside. Don't skip it.
  1. Click Get Started in the invitation email.
  2. Create your account and enter all required information.
  3. Add your SSN or Tax ID# (required even with an LLC or Sole Partnership).
💡 Already have a Wingspan account? Your setup will skip the info-gathering step.

How Wingspan Works

1st
1st of each month

Your Harvest hours are sent to Base and Wingspan. Your invoice becomes available to review.

10th
Deadline — 10th of each month

Log into Wingspan and click Accept Invoice. Missing this deadline may push payment back by one month.

15th
15th of the following month

Payment is issued. Example: July hours are paid on August 15th.

📅 Set a recurring calendar event or Slackbot reminder around the 8th so you never miss the approval deadline.

Disputes & One-Time Payments

If your invoice is incorrect, select Dispute Invoice instead of Accept. Write a detailed message — it goes directly to Base Accounting. Once corrected, return to Wingspan to accept.

⚠️ Disputes filed close to the 10th deadline may cause a payment delay.

Success payments are added automatically — no action needed. Reset Specialists: email talent@basehq.com with your name, assignment, and payment amount once complete.

Contract Renewals & Rate Negotiations

At Base, we're committed to fair, transparent, and competitive compensation. Your rate is based on a consistent, predictable system — not on how well you negotiate.

⚖️
Fair

No negotiation games. Everyone operates on the same consistent criteria.

🔍
Transparent

We operate without secrets. Your earnings are private — shared only when necessary.

📖
Easily Understood

Everyone should know what affects their rate and how to progress over time.

💰
Competitive

Your compensation is on par with or better than similar roles elsewhere.

How to Request a Rate Negotiation

For annual contract renewal, reply directly to your anniversary email. For out-of-cycle negotiations, email talent@basehq.com. Include: your name, client name(s) and block size(s), length of relationship, your hourly rate request, evidence of exceptional performance, a summary of why you feel you deserve a rate increase, and whether you're comfortable with Base contacting your client to confirm feedback (Y/N).

💬 Pay questions? Email talent@basehq.com.

Client Travel Policy

Base doesn't require travel, but supports it when mutually beneficial. Clients are responsible for all travel-related costs.

Before You Travel — Align on the Following

  • Flying or driving? If driving and unable to work, bill transit time in Harvest.
  • Will there be downtime for other clients?
  • Any additional work-related expenses?
  • Does your client have a formal travel policy, per diem, or budget?
✈️ Flights & Transport

Book via the client's card. Consider linking to Uber or Lyft for single-use access.

🚗 Mileage

Reimbursed at the IRS standard rate. Do not also request fuel reimbursement.

🏨 Hotel

Confirm budget upfront. Incidentals (mini-bar, room service) are not reimbursable.

💬 Questions about travel? Contact success@basehq.com.

Expense Reimbursement

All reimbursable expenses are processed through Harvest and Wingspan. Get written approval before incurring any expense.

💡 Expense reimbursements are non-taxable and won't appear on your 1099. All expenses must be logged in Harvest within 30 days.

How to Report an Expense in Harvest

1

Open Harvest and click Expenses from the top menu bar.

2

Click New Expense and select a project.

3

Enter the amount, date, and detailed notes. Attach a photo of your receipt.

4

Select whether the expense is billable. If yes, contact success@basehq.com to confirm.

5

Click Save Expense.

Base is happy to offer standby coverage for your client while you're out. If your vacation is 3 business days or less, coverage likely isn't needed.

💡 You can bill up to 1.5 hours per client in Harvest for vacation coverage planning (Base Internal Requests → Vacation Coverage Planning).

Short-Term Leave

If you're welcoming a new family member, managing a health issue, or need extended time away for personal reasons — you're encouraged to take a short-term leave. Base will work with you to develop a plan, arrange temporary client coverage, and support your return when you're ready.

🌿 When you return, we'll work with you to transition back while keeping any new or changed capacity in mind.
💬 To get started, fill out the short-term leave survey. Questions? Email talent@basehq.com.

Client Transitions

Base's policy is that any EA wishing to end a client engagement provides a 2-week notice directly to Base — before informing the client. We prioritize troubleshooting before approving a transition, but if a transition is needed, we'll support you through it.

EA-Initiated Transitions

1
Inform Base first

Email talent@basehq.com with your client's name, how long you've worked together, your reason, and your ideal last day.

2
Decide when to notify your client

We strongly encourage you to share the news directly — typically during a check-in call.

3
Create an EA–Client Transition Guide

Review and update your client documentation — communication preferences, working style, SOPs, and general notes. The incoming EA will receive this document.

4
Complete the transition

Base will begin matching the client with a new EA and remove you from the client's Harvest project and Base software once complete.

💬 Transitions can be tough. We're here for you throughout. Reach out to talent@basehq.com anytime.

Signing Legal Documents

Legal paperwork is typically handled during the Base sales process. Occasionally, a client may ask you directly to sign something. As a Base contractor, you are not obligated to sign anything.

1
Ask for context

2
Share with Base

Email a copy to success@basehq.com and talent@basehq.com.

3
Proceed only with approval

Base will let you know if it's appropriate for you to sign. You are never required to sign without Base's review and approval.
