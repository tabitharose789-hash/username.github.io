Commitments

Your commitments as a Base EA

We expect you to honor your agreements with Base and the executives you serve. If you foresee any issues meeting a deadline, please communicate ahead of time and propose a new, realistic timeline.

Please note the following EA commitments, outlined in Schedule 2 of your Base EA contractor agreement:

  • Block fulfillment: You are expected to fill your client's monthly block in full, every month. Executives deserve to receive the full level of support they are paying for, and there are endless ways an EA can proactively create value. It is your responsibility to identify opportunities, anticipate needs, and ensure that your client's hours are fully utilized.
    • If more hours are needed, always communicate directly with the client before going over.
  • Minimum commitment: Commit to at least 4 months with each new client (unless the client or Base ends the partnership sooner).
  • Time tracking: Track all hours worked in Harvest.
  • Onboarding plan: Complete the "First 30 Days Onboarding Plan" and deliverables (found in Teachable) when you are matched with a new client.
  • Client paperwork: Some clients require NDAs, data security agreements, or other paperwork. Be prepared to sign what's needed to begin work.
  • Weekly updates: Share hours used with your client weekly to ensure transparency.
  • Transition process: If you are leaving a Base client, inform Base first and follow the transition process outlined in the "Client transitions" section of this course.
  • Stay engaged: Participate in Base community resources (primarily on Slack) to continue your professional learning and to stay connected to your peers.
  • Boost your profile: Client testimonials strengthen your profile and increase your chances of future matches.
    • You are strongly encouraged to include at least one client testimonial on your profile!

How matching works at Base

From your first expression of interest to your kick-off call. Here's the full picture, step by step.

The 5-step matching process

1
Mark your interest

Log into the talent portal and mark your interest in any clients you'd like to work with. There's no limit; our Talent team will only present you to one active client at a time unless you've discussed otherwise.

2
The talent team identifies top matches

Our team reviews your profile, interest statement, time zone, areas of expertise, and client preferences to determine the best fits.

3
The client reviews two EA profiles

The client receives two profiles from our Talent team and decides whether to request a GTKY call. The client may also decline both and request new options at any time.

4
Get-to-know-you (GTKY) calls

If selected by the client, you'll attend a 15-minute GTKY call. These brief conversations gauge chemistry, communication style, and alignment before anyone commits. If it doesn't feel right, that's okay. These calls exist to ensure a mutual fit.

5
The client makes their final selection

After GTKY calls, the client selects their EA, or requests new options until they're ready to move forward.

What happens after?

✅
If you're selected

You'll receive an email notification. Click through to accept the opportunity before moving forward. Once accepted, the Talent team sets up your tools and sends the New client onboarding checklist.

💛
If you're not chosen

You'll still receive an email. You'll remain in the talent cloud and are encouraged to keep marking interest in other clients. Every EA in the cloud is exceptional. Please don't be discouraged.

Kick-off timeline

1
Accept your offer

Click through the offer email and accept. The Talent team will set up your tools and send the New client onboarding checklist.

2
Receive your introduction

Base will introduce you to your executive via email. From this point, scheduling the kick-off call is your responsibility.

3
Schedule the kick-off call

Reply to the introduction email to confirm day and time, then send a calendar invite to your executive with a video conferencing link included.

4
You're off

We completely trust you to manage your client and your work. The Talent team is always available if you need support.

💬 Questions during onboarding? Reach out to talent@basehq.com. We're always happy to help.

A professional headshot is a key part of making a strong first impression. Here's what to keep in mind:

📐 Framing
  • Chest up, looking directly at the camera
  • Even lighting on your face, no shadows
🖼 Background
  • Clean, neutral background
  • Plain wall or nature work well
  • Avoid distracting elements
💡 Lighting
  • Well-lit location
  • Indoors: avoid backlighting
  • Outdoors: find "open shade"
👔 Attire
  • Office-appropriate clothing
  • Avoid busy textures or logos
  • Keep accessories minimal
📁 Format
  • High-resolution (300 ppi)
  • JPEG or PNG, max 8MB

Harvest

Time tracking and billing

Open Harvest ↗

Introduction to Harvest

EAs use Harvest to log hours on client accounts. You'll also use it to bill for new EA set-up, internal Base requests, and vacation coverage for fellow EAs.

💰
You get paid properly

Every hour you log is an hour you're compensated for.

📊
See where your time goes

Harvest data supports informed conversations with your client about priorities.

📋
Clients get reliable reports

Base sends monthly productivity reports on the 7th, powered by your Harvest data.

General guidelines

  • Work in blocks. Minimize interruptions to log larger chunks of focused time.
  • Don't stop your timer between projects. 30 seconds per transition adds up fast.
  • Review daily. One minute a day keeps missing entries from piling up.
💡 Add the Harvest browser extension and desktop app for easy one-click time tracking.

Categorizing and naming your projects

  • Project: Every Base client has a separate Project. Always log to the right one.
  • Task: Use dropdowns to differentiate work type. Need a new category? Email talent@basehq.com.
  • Notes: Be specific but concise. "Boston Conference Logistics" covers flight, car, and hotel. No need for three entries. Add a note to every task.
💡 If your note captures the purpose of the work, it's specific enough.

Managing your client's block

  • Clarify preferences upfront. Is your client comfortable going over their block?
  • Review weekly. Flag concerns on check-in calls before they become issues.
  • Communicate overages before they happen. Never continue work without client awareness.
  • Estimate with a 25% buffer when giving timelines to your client.
  • Don't work for free. Log every hour. Confirm overages before proceeding.
💡 Overcommunication about time is always better than a surprise overage.

Creating a detailed time report

  1. Click Reports, then Detailed Time.
  2. Choose client(s), or leave blank to see all projects.
  3. Filter by project and task as needed.
  4. Select yourself under Team to see only your hours.
  5. Check Include archived items for older projects.
  6. Click Run Report.
💡 Base sends monthly productivity reports on the 7th of each month. Your Harvest data powers them.

How Base invoices clients

Clients are invoiced on the first business day of each month for pre-paid hour blocks (20-160+ hrs). Unused hours don't roll over. Overages bill the following month. All hours must be logged by EOD on the last day of the month.

💡 Always confirm client approval before working additional time beyond their block.

Frequently asked questions

Why do we use Harvest if we also invoice separately?

Harvest powers accurate monthly invoicing, productivity reports, and seamless project coverage across Base EAs without billing delays.

Is time spent approving my invoice billable?

No. As a 1099 contractor, invoicing is a service for your business, not the client. Since invoices auto-generate through Wingspan, it typically takes just a few minutes.

Getting started with Wingspan

Wingspan is Base's invoicing platform for contractor payments, plus access to health insurance and financial services. Once matched with your first client, look for this email:

📧 "Base invited you to onboard via Wingspan". Your invitation link is inside. Don't skip it.
  1. Click Get started in the invitation email.
  2. Create your account and enter all required information.
  3. Add your SSN or Tax ID# (required even with an LLC or Sole Partnership).
💡 Already have a Wingspan account? Your setup will skip the info-gathering step.

How Wingspan works

1st
1st of each month

Your Harvest hours are sent to Base and Wingspan. Your invoice becomes available to review.

10th
Deadline: 10th of each month

Log into Wingspan and click Accept Invoice. Missing this deadline may push payment back by one month.

15th
15th of the following month

Payment is issued. Example: July hours are paid on August 15th.

📅 Set a recurring calendar event or Slackbot reminder around the 8th so you never miss the approval deadline.

Disputes and one-time payments

If your invoice is incorrect, select Dispute invoice instead of Accept. Write a detailed message. It goes directly to Base Accounting. Once corrected, return to Wingspan to accept.

⚠️ Disputes filed close to the 10th deadline may cause a payment delay.

Success payments are added automatically. No action needed. Reset Specialists: email talent@basehq.com with your name, assignment, and payment amount once complete.

Client travel policy

While Base does not require EAs to travel, we support and encourage it when mutually beneficial. If you're open to traveling, it's important to establish clear expectations before any booking or planning begins. Below are key guidelines for travel-related work.

✈️ Before you travel

Set expectations early by gathering key details from your client. Consider asking:

  • Will you be flying or driving?
    • If driving (and unable to work), bill travel time in Harvest under the client project.
    • If working during transit (e.g. layovers), bill only for time actually worked.
  • Will there be downtime to support other clients?
  • Will you incur additional work-related expenses (e.g. hotel Wi-Fi)?
  • Does your client have a formal travel policy or budget?
    • Ask about per diem, flight/hotel budgets, rental car needs, and daily work expectations.

Clients are responsible for all travel-related expenses. Whenever possible, use the client's credit card for bookings. Review the "Expense reimbursements" section of this course for more information.

Best practices as you prepare to travel for your client:

  • Flights and transportation: Book via the client's card. Consider linking it to a ride-sharing account (Uber, Lyft) for single-use access.
  • Mileage: If you are driving your own car for client travel, you should be reimbursed for mileage. You can see mileage rates here. If you are claiming mileage, you should not also request reimbursement for fuel.
  • Hotel: Confirm the client's budget and use a credit card authorization form if needed.
    • Note that you may still have to present your own credit card at check-in to cover any incidentals or extra charges.
      • While items like parking and Wi-Fi can be billed to the client, incidentals (such as pay-per-view, mini-bar, and room service) are not reimbursable.
  • Meals: Follow the client's per diem or guidance. Be respectful with client-funded purchases.
  • Change fees: Clients cover any changes due to circumstances outside your control (e.g. cancelled flight requiring an extra night at a hotel).
💡 If you support multiple clients, treat travel days like time off. Communicate your availability with all of your clients and arrange EA coverage as needed.

🧭 During travel

Be sure to track expenses and keep receipts, including written records of cash purchases. Throughout your trip, log time in Harvest per your agreement with the client. Generally:

✅ Billable time
  • Meetings, direct client work, event prep, business meals (e.g. supporting at dinners), Base-related tasks
  • Transit time only if you can't work (e.g. driving)
⏸ Non-billable time
  • Optional social events (happy hours, informal meals)
  • Personal time
  • Transit time, if you choose not to work

📬 After travel

If you have paid for any travel expenses that need reimbursement, please follow the "Expense reimbursements" section of this course. In addition, be sure to send your total expense details to your client directly.

Expense reimbursement

It is possible that you may be asked to purchase a program or resource needed for your work. All reimbursable expenses, both internal and client, are processed through Harvest and Wingspan.

🏢 Internal Base expenses

Written approval from a Leadership Team member must be provided prior to incurring an internal expense or requesting expense reimbursement. Once you've received approval, please follow the expense reporting process below.

👤 Client expenses

Sometimes you will have an expense that the client should reimburse (e.g. a subscription to a tool they've asked you to use, shipping charges for a project). Often you will be reimbursed directly through your client and their company.

In certain circumstances, Base will allow client reimbursements. Please request the client's approval in writing before incurring the expense. Once you've received approval, forward the email confirmation to success@basehq.com (we will have the expense applied to the client's next invoice) and follow the expense reporting process below.

How to report any expense

Once written approval has been provided and you have incurred the expense, you must log the expense, the Base approval email, and the receipt into Harvest within 30 days. You can add this information as a customized project in Harvest. All expense reimbursements are processed at the same time as the monthly invoice on which they appear.

💡 Expense reimbursements are non-taxable and will not be included in your 1099 form.

Instructions on reporting an expense

Open Harvest.

Choose a project. "Base Projects" is fine if there is not a better client alignment.

4

Choose an expense category that fits the expense type.

5

Enter expense amount, date, and detailed notes. Include a photo of the receipt.

6

Select whether or not the expense is billable (i.e., reimbursable by the client).

Vacation planning guide

Base is happy to offer the option of "standby" coverage for your client while you're out-of-office. If your client would like support from another Base EA, it's important to set the right expectations around "standby" coverage so they're aware of how to use the back-up EA in your absence.

NOTE: If your vacation is less than one week long, you may not need to offer coverage. We recommend that you do not offer coverage if you will be absent for 3 business days or less.

⏱ Tracking time

  • You can bill up to 1.5 hours per client in Harvest. (Base Internal Requests > Vacation coverage planning)
    • Do not log this time under your client's Harvest project.
  • Your back-up EA can also bill up to 1.5 hours to the same Harvest project for their time spent preparing to provide coverage.

📋 Documentation

Please ensure that you provide documentation to the EA covering your work so that you can enjoy interruption-free time off.

Client transitions

It is Base's policy that any EA who wishes to terminate work on a Base client account provides a 2-week notice directly to Base. To do so, send an email to both success@basehq.com and talent@basehq.com outlining the details and reasons behind the request (more information below).

We aim to match clients and EAs for long-term compatibility, but if challenges arise, we prioritize troubleshooting before approving a transition. If a transition is needed, the 2-week notice begins on the next business day, assuming Base's internal team is available to receive the request.

If an EA would like to transition away from their client, the EA must notify Base before informing the client. If a transition is initiated by the client, Base will manage the process directly.

Select a transition type to view the full process:

EA-initiated

Transitions initiated by the EA

Follow each step below to ensure a smooth handoff for both you and your client.

1
Inform Base of your decision

Send an email to talent@basehq.com and provide the following details:

  • Your client's full name and company
  • How long you've been working with the client
  • The reason you'd like to transition away from your client
  • The ideal date you'd like to be done working with the client
2
Decide when to notify the client

We strongly encourage you to be the one to share the transition news with your client. Typically, EAs share their news during a check-in call.

  • Once you've determined the date and time you'll inform the client, send that information to talent@basehq.com and success@basehq.com.
  • If you're uncomfortable personally sharing the news, email both talent@basehq.com and success@basehq.com to let Base know.

Regardless of who notifies the client, it's crucial for Base to stay informed and in the loop. This enables us to coordinate our communications with clients effectively.

3
Notify your client

On the call with your client, be sure to discuss the following:

  • Your last day working with the client
  • Any outstanding projects you intend to complete
  • Next steps with Base:
    • You will schedule a call for your client to discuss next steps with Base (see Step 4)
    • Base will send over a client match form for the client to fill out
    • Base will prioritize their matching process to ensure seamless coverage
4
Schedule a call between your client and Base

Use this booking link to schedule a 30-minute call with your client and Base's Success team to discuss next steps. Let the client know that Base is happy to answer any questions they may have on this call.

5
Create an EA to client transition guide

Review and update the current client information you have on file, including communication preferences, working styles, calendar and travel preferences, SOPs, and general documentation.

  • Make a copy of the EA to Client Transition Guide and fill it out to the best of your ability.
  • This document will be shared with the incoming EA.
6
Complete the transition process

Once your client is informed and the transition process is underway, the matching team will start looking for a new EA for the client.

  • Any information you've shared with Base will be shared with the incoming EA.
  • If you have insights about the client that could be helpful to the matching team, please share them with talent@basehq.com.
  • If you plan to onboard the incoming EA (as noted in your transition guide), they will reach out to you directly to get started.
  • Base will reach out to you for any additional requests, if necessary.

In terms of client offboarding tasks:

  • Base will remove you from your client's Harvest project and Base software workspace once the transition is complete.
  • Any additional offboarding steps should be discussed directly with your client, since this typically involves closing out existing projects and shutting down tool access.
7
Prepare for a new client match

If you're interested in being matched with a new client, log into the talent portal to update your EA profile (including your updated capacity for client work).

  • You may also want to consider adding professional certificates, sample projects, and testimonials to your profile.

If you have any questions or need additional support throughout this process, please reach out to talent@basehq.com.

💬 We understand that transitions can be tough, and we want you to feel supported throughout. Please reach out to talent@basehq.com with any questions or concerns regarding your transition.
